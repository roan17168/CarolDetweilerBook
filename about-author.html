A Lifetime of Crossing Borders

Carol Detweiler has had a lengthy international and intercultural career, beginning with work as a Peace Corps Volunteer, and subsequently working with international students and as a volunteer with organizations involved in global education. She has traveled to more than fifty countries, but one destination changed her life forever.

In 1999, having heard that refugees would be allowed to enter the U.S. from war-torn Kosova, and knowing some would be of college age, Carol immediately established a program to provide full college scholarships for more than 50 Kosovars, allowing them to continue their education.

In recognition of this achievement, she was honored by the New York State Senate with a "Women of Distinction" award in 2000.

This book is based on the experiences of these refugees, as well as interviews with many others—including Kosova's president, Fatmir Sejdiu. Having made numerous trips to Kosova, along with extensive research into the region's history and the war, she has sought through this historical fiction to bring alive the character of the Kosovar people and the love they have for their country.

Why I Wrote This Book

"When my students arrived in America, they carried one bag of clothes and a lifetime of stories no history book had recorded. I didn't do the work for them — I simply unlocked a heavy iron door they had to push through themselves."

"Through historical fiction, I sought to bring alive the character of the Kosovar people and the profound love they hold for their country."

"This book is my way of making sure the door stays open, and the stories keep walking through. It is my thank-you to the friends who trusted me with their memories, and my invitation to readers who might never otherwise open a history book about the Balkans."

— Carol

Life Beyond the Page

Carol has lived across the United States — California, New Jersey, upstate New York, Washington D.C., and Michigan — and now resides in Oregon with her husband, Richard Detweiler, and their standard poodle. They have two daughters, a son, and seven grandchildren.
