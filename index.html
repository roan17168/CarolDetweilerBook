A Story of Survival

In the shadow of the Balkan Mountains, escalating ethnic tensions erupt into the NATO-led war against Serbia in 1999. Two families—one Catholic, one Muslim—are torn apart by violence, displacement, and the unbearable cost of survival.

Told in the unflinching first-person voice of Ariana Hoxha, this Kosova war historical fiction is a testament to courage unyielding and spirit unbroken.

More Than a Novel. A Movement.

In 1999, Carol Detweiler didn't just witness the Kosovar refugee crisis—she answered it. She created a scholarship program that sent more than 50 young Kosovars to American colleges, unlocking futures that war had tried to steal.
