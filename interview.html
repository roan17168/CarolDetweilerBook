In Her Own Words

Conversations with Carol Detweiler

Journey into the heart of A Murder of Crows Fly with a Red Ribbon through this deeply moving conversation between Carol Detweiler and Kushtrim Hoxha — a former Kosovar refugee turned American actor whose life story mirrors the very themes of the novel.

Key Moments — Jump to Chapter

Click any timestamp to jump directly to that moment in the interview.

  • 04:02

    The One-Bag Reality

    Carol reflects on seeing refugee students arrive at college with a single bag of belongings — while American students unloaded carloads of luggage.

  • 05:18

    The Invisible Baggage

    A powerful discussion of the emotional weight and unspoken trauma that refugees carry long after they've reached safety.

  • 06:21

    Unlocking the Iron Door

    Carol shares her guiding metaphor: her role wasn't to do the work for her students, but to unlock a heavy iron door that they alone had the strength to push through.

  • 10:38

    From Refugee Camp to Actor

    Kushtrim Hoxha tells his own story — arriving in Macedonia in 1999 with "one shirt" and building a career on the American stage.

  • 30:13

    The Symbolism of the Red Ribbon

    Carol explains the title's meaning: the "murder of crows" as the terror of the battlefield, the "red ribbon" as the hope, blood, and memory of a people who refused to disappear.
