In Her Own Words

Conversations with Carol Detweiler

Journey into the heart of A Murder of Crows Fly with a Red Ribbon through this deeply moving conversation between Carol Detweiler and Kushtrim Hoxha — a former Kosovar refugee turned American actor whose life story mirrors the very themes of the novel.

Key Moments — Jump to Chapter

Click any timestamp to jump directly to that moment in the interview.

  • 04:02

    The One-Bag Reality

    Carol reflects on seeing refugee students arrive at college with a single bag of belongings — while American students unloaded carloads of luggage.

  • 05:18

    The Invisible Baggage

    Carol discusses the emotional and psychological trauma students brought with them, far heavier than the physical bags they carried.

  • 06:21

    Unlocking the Iron Door

    Carol uses the metaphor of a "heavy iron door" to describe the opportunities she helped unlock, which the students then had to push through themselves.

  • 10:38

    From Refugee Camp to Actor

    Kushtrim shares his personal journey of being an acting student in Pristina when the war broke out, leading him eventually to the United States.

  • 30:13

    The Symbolism of the Red Ribbon

    Carol explains the dual meaning behind the book's title, representing both the terror of war and the red ribbon of hope and optimism.

  • 39:50

    The Agony of Choice

    Carol shares a heartbreaking story of two sisters who forfeited their scholarships to work in a factory, highlighting the agonizing choices refugee families face.

  • 44:50

    The Bitter Burning Pages

    Carol reads a visceral passage about the destruction of Albanian culture through the burning of books, comparing knowledge to the "bitter ash" of lost history.

  • 52:19

    Crossing Into Freedom

    A reading from the book explores how reaching safety is only the beginning, as refugees carry heavy emotional baggage across the bridge to their new lives.

  • 56:30

    The Spirit of Hospitality

    Carol recounts her 2004 visit to Kosovo, expressing her profound awe at the "extraordinary beauty" and legendary hospitality of the people.
